4 · sanity-check our thinking

Is the order we apply rules right?

We started by treating the dollar amount as the first question for everything. Reading the rules, that looks wrong: for a lot of purchases the commodity decides the answer and price is a side issue. So we reordered it to three layers, top to bottom. Does this match reality? Correct or add to it in the note.
How we now think it resolves
1 · Mandatory contract / channel first. If the item is on a statewide term contract or a required punchout (Staples, Dell/Apple/HP, Print Services, Aramark…), you buy from it — regardless of price. This can replace getting quotes/bids. e.g. $30k of office supplies → Staples contract, not a sealed bid.

2 · Then commodity & funding requirements stack on top. IT → CCIT CheckIT; alcohol → Aramark waiver; hazmat rules; federal grant → 2 CFR 200 clauses. These add steps; they don't replace the method.

3 · Dollar amount decides the competition method last — micro-purchase vs. quotes vs. IFB vs. sole source — and only when no mandatory contract already settled it.
