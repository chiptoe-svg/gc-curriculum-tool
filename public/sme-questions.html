A few questions from the team building a procurement assistant

Help us get the rules right.

We're building a tool that answers "how do I buy this compliantly?" for Clemson faculty and staff — and it must use real, current rules with citations, never guesses. We're developers, not procurement experts, so we need you to confirm a handful of specifics. Answer whatever you can; skip the rest. Even partial answers help a lot.

No wrong answers, and you don't need to be exhaustive — a sentence, a link, or "see this PDF" is perfect. Where you can, point us at the authoritative source (a page or document) so the tool can cite it.
Q1 — most useful

What are the authoritative sources we should read?

The exact web pages / PDFs that are the real rulebook — Clemson procurement policy, the SC state procurement code/manual, and anything for grant-funded (federal) purchases. Links or document names.
Q2

How are purchases categorized?

In BuyWays (or however you classify), what are the main categories of things people buy? Especially: which categories follow special rules (e.g. IT, construction, professional/consulting services, furniture, equipment)?
Q3 — the core

What are the dollar thresholds, and what method does each require?

The bands that change what someone must do — e.g. "under $X → P-Card", "$X–$Y → get N quotes", "over $Z → formal bid/RFP". Please give the current numbers for Clemson and for SC state if they differ. If there's a table somewhere, a link is great.
Q4

Who has to approve, and what documents are required, at each level?

e.g. department head vs. business officer vs. CPO; and what must be on file (quotes summary, sole-source justification, scope of work, etc.).
Q5

Exceptions — sole source and emergency.

When do the normal rules NOT apply? What qualifies as sole-source or emergency, and what justification/paperwork is needed?
Q6

Grant / federal money — what changes?

When a purchase is on a federal grant (Uniform Guidance / 2 CFR 200), how do the rules differ? Do you apply the stricter of state vs. federal? Any thresholds specific to grants?
Q7

What does the tool absolutely have to get right?

The mistakes you see people make, or the questions you answer over and over. This tells us where accuracy matters most.
