# Doignon & Falmagne (1985) — Spaces for the Assessment of Knowledge

**Full citation:** Doignon, J.-P., & Falmagne, J.-C. (1985). Spaces for the assessment of knowledge. *International Journal of Man-Machine Studies*, 23(2), 175–196. Math. Psych.* 41(3), 275–279).

**What the background doc claims it says:** The founding paper of Knowledge Space Theory (KST) — a domain modeled as items linked by a surmise (prerequisite-like) relation, with "knowledge states" (sets of mastered items) and their fringes; the formal cousin of a curriculum's prerequisite-graph-with-depth.

**What it actually says:** Introduces knowledge structures/spaces as families of subsets of a question domain, closed under union, built from a surmise relation/system. A learner's knowledge state is the set of items they can solve; the outer fringe is what they are ready to learn next.
