# NACE (annual) — Job Outlook

**Full citation:** National Association of Colleges and Employers. *Job Outlook*. Annual employer survey of competencies most desired in college graduates; problem-solving consistently rates among the top five attributes. https://www.naceweb.org/research/job-outlook.

**What the background doc claims it says:** Problem-solving consistently rates among the top five attributes employers look for in college graduates.

**What it actually says (synthesis):** The NACE (National Association of Colleges and Employers) Job Outlook is an annual survey of employer members asking about hiring intentions, desired attributes, and career readiness competency ratings for new college graduates. It is a widely cited source in career development and higher education discussions.

Based on 2023–2024 data: Nearly 90% of employers indicated they are seeking evidence of a student's ability to solve problems — ranking problem-solving as the top or near-top desired competency on graduate resumes. The top attributes employers report seeking when reviewing resumes consistently include: problem-solving skills, ability to work in a team, written communication skills, strong work ethic, and flexibility/adaptability. Problem-solving has been ranked in the top five attributes in every recent survey.

NACE separately tracks "career readiness competencies" (a different list from resume attributes) which includes Critical Thinking/Problem Solving as one of eight defined competencies. Employers consistently rate Critical Thinking/Problem Solving as one of the two most important competencies (along with Professionalism/Work Ethic), and also note a gap between how important they consider it and how proficient they find recent graduates to be.

The background doc cites NACE in §4 as the basis for the claim that "problem-solving is among the attributes employers most consistently value when reviewing graduates' resumes — repeatedly ranked at the top of NACE's annual Job Outlook resume-attribute surveys, alongside teamwork and written communication." This accurately reflects the survey's findings.

In §8, the NACE Career Readiness Competencies framework (eight competencies: Critical Thinking, Communication, Teamwork, Technology, Leadership, Professionalism, Career & Self-Development, Equity & Inclusion) is cited as one of three influences on the selection of the five baseline foundational competencies.

**Verdict:** Consistent. The doc's claims about NACE findings are accurate. Problem-solving does consistently rank at or near the top of the attributes employers seek in graduates' resumes in NACE surveys. The doc parenthetically notes "(NACE, 2023)" for a specific survey year, which is appropriate given the annual nature of the publication.
