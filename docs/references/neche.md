# NECHE (2015) — Policy Statement on Competency-Based Education

**Full citation:** New England Commission of Higher Education. (2015). *Policy Statement on Competency-Based Education*.

**What the background doc claims it says:** Used as the accreditation-policy formulation of competency-based education (CBE) in §2; states that KUD+ shares the CBE mastery orientation in its depth scales and the external-alignment orientation through the career-path layer. The framework departs from strict CBE in two ways: it does not propose self-paced credit, and it treats program-level mapping as the primary deliverable rather than individual transcripts of demonstrated competencies.

**What it actually says (synthesis):** The NECHE (formerly NEASC) 2015 policy statement was a regional accreditors' collaborative statement on how CBE programs can qualify for accreditation under regional standards that were originally designed for credit-hour-based programs. It was one of several regional accreditor statements issued around 2014–2015 in response to the growth of direct-assessment CBE programs at institutions like Western Governors University, Capella University, and Southern New Hampshire University.

Based on the broader regional accreditation CBE guidance from this period (NECHE is one of seven regional accreditors that issued similar statements), the key points covered in such documents typically include: defining CBE as a model in which students advance upon demonstrated mastery of competencies rather than completion of seat time; clarifying that CBE is compatible with Title IV financial aid eligibility under certain conditions; identifying what quality assurance practices must be in place (faculty oversight of competency definitions, assessment validity, rigor alignment to degree level); and distinguishing "direct assessment" CBE (which abandons credit hours entirely) from "hybrid" CBE (which maps competencies onto conventional credit-hour structures).

The document does not prescribe any specific framework for measuring depth of competence; it establishes the accreditation conditions under which CBE programs can operate — not a taxonomy or measurement tool.

**Verdict:** Consistent. The background doc uses this reference narrowly — as the accreditation-policy formulation of CBE — and correctly notes that KUD+ shares CBE's mastery orientation without adopting its structural features (self-paced credit, individual competency transcripts). This is an accurate characterization of the relationship between the framework and CBE as a policy-defined alternative credentialing model. The reference is a policy document, not a research source, and is cited appropriately as such.
