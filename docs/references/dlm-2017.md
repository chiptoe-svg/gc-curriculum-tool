# Kingston & Broaddus (2017) — Learning Map Systems (Dynamic Learning Maps)

**Full citation:** Kingston, N. M., & Broaddus, A. (2017). The use of learning map systems to support the formative assessment in mathematics. *Education Sciences*, 7(1), Article 41. DOI: 10.3390/educsci7010041.

**Reference ID in background.html:** `ref-dlm-2017`; cited in §2.

**Where it lives:**
- URL: https://www.mdpi.com/2227-7102/7/1/41 (and ERIC EJ1135002)
- Open-access status: Open access (MDPI).

**Accessibility:** Fully open. Bibliographic fields confirmed at MDPI. The figure "2,554 nodes and 5,605 connections" is verbatim in the paper (text p. 4 and the Figure 3 caption), referring specifically to the University of Kansas **mathematics** learning map.

**What the background doc claims it says:** K–12 learning-map systems (the Dynamic Learning Maps project) represent a subject as a large network of competency nodes connected by skill-to-skill links; its mathematics map has 2,554 nodes and 5,605 connections.

**What it actually says:** Describes the DLM learning-map model and its use for formative assessment in mathematics, characterizing the map as "nodes" (aspects of mathematical knowledge) and "connections" among skills, with alternative pathways.

**Adversarial check (2026-06-05):** Node/connection **counts** — verbatim-correct. Framing — the original draft called it "a literal directed acyclic graph … connected by prerequisite edges"; the adversarial verifier found that Kingston & Broaddus (2017) **never** use "directed acyclic graph"/"DAG" or "prerequisite," do not prove acyclicity, and describe edges only as "connections." (The DAG / prerequisite characterization is supported by other DLM technical material but not by *this* paper.) Prose corrected to "a large network of competency nodes connected by skill-to-skill links." Also noted that the cited counts are the math map specifically, not the whole DLM project.

**Verdict:** Corrected. Counts accurate; graph-theoretic terminology removed from the attribution.
